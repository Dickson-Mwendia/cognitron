## Coach Profiles & Safeguarding (DEC-010)

### Coach Profiles: ❌ NOT IMPLEMENTED

**What DEC-010 specified:** Every coach must have a visible website profile with 12 required fields (photo, bio, qualifications, teaching experience, background check badge, specializations, etc.).

**What exists now:**
- About page: Dickson Mwendia listed as "Founder & Lead Coach" with title "Software Engineer" and "Microsoft" affiliation. LinkedIn linked. Avatar shows initials "DM" — no real photo.
- Homepage: "Every Cognitron coach is a practising software engineer at a leading tech company. They coach exclusively after work hours."
- No dedicated /coaches or /team page.
- No qualifications, no teaching experience, no background check badge, no photo.

**Grace's reaction:** "This man is coming to my home to spend 90 minutes alone with my children, and all I know is he works at Microsoft? Where's his photo? What's his teaching background? Has he been vetted? I wouldn't hire a plumber with this little information."

### Safeguarding Policy: ❌ NOT IMPLEMENTED

**What DEC-010 specified:** A public safeguarding statement is non-negotiable for a home-visit service targeting minors. Should include: commitment statement, background check requirements, code of conduct, reporting procedures, parent rights.

**What exists now:**
- `/protect` pages are about cybersecurity services (Family Digital Safety, Executive Cyber Protection) — NOT child safeguarding.
- `/privacy` page covers data protection under Kenya DPA 2019, including children's privacy protections. Excellent and comprehensive.
- No dedicated safeguarding page or statement.
- No mention of background checks, Certificate of Good Conduct, DBS, or child protection policies anywhere on the marketing site.

**Grace's reaction:** "There's a privacy policy for data — good. But where's the safeguarding policy for my actual child? You're sending someone to my home. I need to see your child protection policy before I give you my address."

## Top 5 Trust Gaps (What Would Stop Grace from Signing Up)

### 1. 🔴 No Coach Profile or Photo (CRITICAL — unchanged from DEC-009)

A stranger is coming to Grace's home to be alone with her children. She can see "Software Engineer, Microsoft" and a circle with initials "DM." No photo. No teaching qualifications. No background check badge. No years of experience with children. No other coaches listed.

**For a premium home-visit service targeting minors, this is a deal-breaker.** Grace would message the WhatsApp but she would NOT give her home address until she's seen a face, credentials, and evidence of vetting.

**Fix:** Implement coach profiles page per DEC-010 spec. At minimum: real photo, teaching qualifications, years of experience, Certificate of Good Conduct badge, short bio. This is 2 hours of content work and 4 hours of dev work.

### 2. 🔴 No Safeguarding Policy (CRITICAL — unchanged from DEC-009)

Grace is a doctor. She thinks about child protection professionally. She will look for a safeguarding policy. The `/protect` page is about cybersecurity, not child safety. The privacy policy covers data, not physical safety.

There is no statement anywhere on the site about:
- Background checks for coaches
- Code of conduct for home visits
- What happens if a child reports a concern
- Parent rights during sessions
- Who a parent can report concerns to

**For a home-visit service, this is the legal and moral baseline.** Not having it doesn't just lose Grace — it's a liability.

**Fix:** Publish safeguarding page at `/safeguarding` per DEC-010 spec. Coach conduct code, background check requirements, reporting procedure, parent rights. This is 1 day of content work.

### 3. 🟡 Session Duration Not Stated Anywhere (IMPORTANT)

The most basic question — "how long is each session?" — is unanswered on every page of the site. The homepage, pricing page, FAQ, how-it-works page, track pages — none state "90 minutes." Grace literally cannot plan her Saturday if she doesn't know when the coach will leave.

**Fix:** Add "90-minute sessions" to: pricing page features, FAQ, how-it-works steps, and each track page. 30 minutes of dev work.

### 4. 🟡 No Real Photography (IMPORTANT — unchanged from DEC-009)

Every image on the site is either an icon, an avatar with initials, or decorative. No photos of:
- A real coaching session
- Children coding/playing chess
- The coach working with students
- A Nairobi home setting

The site still feels like a polished wireframe. Premium Nairobi parents are used to seeing real imagery from schools and activity providers. Stock photos would be better than nothing, but real session photography (as specified in DEC-008) would transform credibility.

**Fix:** Execute the photo shoot brief from DEC-008. In the meantime, even 3-4 phone photos from a real session would be better than zero imagery.

### 5. 🟡 Dashboard is Mostly "Coming Soon" (MODERATE)

A parent who signs up, gets approved, and reaches the dashboard will find:
- ✅ Working: Child cards, add-child, XP/streaks display, coach notes, upcoming sessions
- ⚠️ Coming soon: Progress reports, manage children, messages, schedule, billing

That's 5 of 8 features as stubs. The first impression is good, but clicking through reveals the platform isn't ready for daily use. Grace would wonder if she's paying full price for a half-built product.

**Fix:** Prioritise progress reports (most requested) and messages (coach communication is critical). The other stubs can wait.
