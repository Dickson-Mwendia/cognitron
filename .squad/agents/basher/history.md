**Key Marketing Decisions:**
1. **WhatsApp-first strategy** — Nairobi parents live on WhatsApp, not email. Broadcast lists (not groups) for marketing to avoid spam feel.
2. **Founding Family urgency** — 10 slots only, locked-in pricing forever, lifetime priority booking, quarterly coach reports. Creates FOMO without feeling scammy.
3. **Pre-shoot content workarounds** — Canva graphics, text-based posts, chess puzzles, coding tips, behind-the-scenes setup shots. No excuse to delay launch while waiting for professional photography.
4. **Local SEO dominance** — Google Business Profile is FREE and high-ROI for local services. Optimize hard (Q&A, photos, posts, reviews).
5. **Referral as growth engine** — LTV of KES 811k per family means we can afford generous referral rewards. Make existing families our sales team.
6. **Budget prioritization** — 50%+ of Month 1 budget to content creation (photo/video shoot). You can't scale without assets. Paid ads are secondary until organic channels are optimized.

**Nairobi-Specific Insights:**
- **Tone matters** — Formal corporate messaging alienates Nairobi parents (especially mums). They respond to warmth, authenticity, "I'm a parent too" energy.
- **Neighborhoods as identity** — Karen/Lavington/Runda parents self-identify by area. Use neighborhood names everywhere (landing pages, ads, testimonials, events).
- **Timing is cultural** — WhatsApp messages at 9-10:30am (post school drop-off) or 7-8:30pm (post-dinner) get highest response. Fridays are mentally checked out. Sundays are family time (good for inspiration, bad for hard sales).
- **Trust = everything** — Safeguarding, background checks, coach credentials must be front-and-center. Parents won't let strangers into their homes without serious vetting proof.
- **Pricing transparency** — Don't hide KES amounts or use "contact us for pricing." Affluent Nairobi parents appreciate transparency (builds trust). Premium pricing is fine if justified.
- **Social proof currency** — "5 families from Karen have enrolled" > any ad copy. Names drop neighborhoods, not just "families in Nairobi."
- **Events > ads** — Founding Families Meet & Greet at a Karen home builds community + trust faster than KES 50k in Instagram ads. In-person matters in this demographic.

**What Works in This Market:**
- Home-visit convenience (Nairobi traffic is hell — parents will pay premium to avoid driving to Westlands for classes)
- 1-on-1 personalization (group classes = kids get lost in the crowd, slower progress)
- Future-proof skills narrative (parents applying to ISK/Brookhouse/schools abroad care about "what sets my kid apart")
- Chess as brain training (not just a game — resonates with education-obsessed parents)
- Founder-led brand (personal story > corporate messaging)
